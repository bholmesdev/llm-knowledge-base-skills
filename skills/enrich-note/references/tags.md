# Tag registry

The canonical list of topic tags. `enrich-note` reads this before tagging.

**Rules**

- Reuse an existing tag whenever it fits. Coin a new one only when nothing matches.
- Keep the set small. Prefer a broad existing tag over a narrow new one.
- When you coin a tag, add it here with a one-line description, kebab-case, before using it.
- Topics only. Lifecycle tags (e.g. `voice-transcription`) do **not** belong here.

## Faith & scripture

- `faith` — Christian belief, theology, and the life of faith (default for any religious note)
- `apologetics` — the historical/evidential case for Christianity (Case for Christ, resurrection, historical Jesus)
- `bible-study` — study of scripture: the prophets, Old Testament books, specific passages

## Anthropology & society

- `anthropology` — culture, evolution, social norms, how societies work
- `secret-of-our-success` — notes from *The Secret of Our Success*

## Tech & AI

- `ai` — AI models, tools, and their impact
- `ai-hardware` — GPUs, chips, accelerators, Nvidia
- `claude-code` — coding agents, agent loops, code review bots, AI-assisted dev
- `open-source` — open-source strategy and community
- `software-engineering` — general software engineering: coding, tooling, system design, dev setup, technical interviews

## Product & business

- `hubble` — the Hubble notes app: product, features, dev
- `product-design` — product/UX thinking
- `startup` — building a business: strategy, go-to-market, PLG, pricing
- `founders` — lessons from founders and the craft of building companies

## Creativity & craft

- `creativity` — making, taste, the creative process, simplicity
- `writing` — writing, communication, and content for an audience
- `music` — music and music production

## Growth & living

- `self-improvement` — habits, mindset, focus, personal growth
- `relationships` — connection, love, friendship

## Literature

- `literature` — reflections on novels and stories

## Personal & misc

- `lists` — personal lists and references (books, places, errands)

## Source medium (apply when the note has an origin)

- `book`
